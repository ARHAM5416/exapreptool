We’re often asked what we want to become in our life and we answered variedly and yes, these answers also changed throughout our academic lives and with the passage of time. But right after graduation, the time actually comes when we have to fulfil that answer with our actions as now even we ask ourselves- “what I want to become in my Life?”

Being in India we have many options at our hand depending upon our qualifications, age and area of interests or expertise. So, here we are to get you acquainted with quite a few such exams that you can take after getting done with your graduation. Although the eligibility criteria could be way more vast but we are trying to fetch you with the most important information of qualification and age requirement for the below mentioned exams.

1. Civil Services Examination:

Who is not aware of this exam? Yes, this is one of the most difficult exams that thousands of aspirants write every year. It is conducted by UPSC. The official link for the same is www.upsc.gov.in . The qualification requirements for this exam are mentioned below-

  • The candidate must be a graduate from-

    • Any university or institution which is incorporated by an act of the Central or State Legislature in India or if it is a deemed university then it should be established as per the Act of Parliament as per the UGC Act, 1956.

  • If the candidate is a final year student then he is eligible to appear for the Preliminary Examination but they ought to submit their passing certificate before appearing for the Mains.

    • Candidates from any stream of subject are eligible to apply for this examination and also there is no minimum bar for the percentage of marks to be secured in the graduation. The only crucial requirement is that- the bachelor’s degree so obtained has to be from a recognised and certified institute or university by the Indian Government.

    Age limit and the number of attempts are given below-

    Category Age limit (maximum) Attempts (maximum)
    General 32 years 2
    Other Backward Castes (OBC)/th> 35 years 9
    Economically Weaker Section (EWS) 32 years 6
    Scheduled Caste/Tribes (SC/ST) 37 years They can appear until they’re of 37 years
    Physically Disabled Candidates (blind, deaf, mute, orthopaedic) 42 years For general and OBC- 9 attempts and for SC/ST- they can appear until they turn overage

    2. Indian Economic Service Examination:

    The Indian Economic Service Examination is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission. It is conducted once every year. It is an offline exam and is of 3 days.

    Its official website is www.upsc.gov.in .

    Its qualification requirements necessarily includes that the candidate must possess a post-graduation degree in Economics/ Applied Economics/ Business Economics/ Econometrics from a university or an institute or even a foreign institute/university recognised by the Indian Government.

    As far as age limits are concerned then the candidate must fall within the age bracket of 21 to 30 years. Further relaxations are also there for different categories which you can check from the given link of the official website.

    3. Indian Statistical Service Examination:

    The Indian Statistical Service Examination is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission. It takes place once in a year. It is also an offline examination and the official website for the same is www.upsc.gov.in .

    The qualification requirements state that the candidate must be in the possession of a Bachelors’ Degree with his/her subject as statistics/ mathematical statistics/applied statistics. Candidates who have completed their post-graduation with statistics/mathematical statistics/ applied statistics as one of their subjects are also eligible to apply for this examination. The concerned university/institute can be an Indian or a foreign but has to be recognised by the Indian Government.

    The eligible age brackets are also the same as they are for Indian Economic Service Examination which is- 21years to 30years. The candidates can look for further age relaxations concerned with different categories from the official website via the link given above.

    4.Combined Defence Services Examination:

    Combined Defence Services Examination is yet another examination which is conducted by Union Public Services Commission. It is conducted twice a year and is a gateway to join Indian Armed Forces namely- Indian Army, Indian Airforce, Indian Navy and Officers Training Academy. Official website for this exam is www.upsc.gov.in .

    Preparation strategy for SBI-PO Mains:
    1. In order to apply for the India Military Academy and Officers’ Training Academy (OTA), the candidate must be a graduate from any recognized university or institute.
    2. In order to apply for Indian Airforce Academy, the candidate must be a graduate with Physics and Mathematics as her/his subjects in 12th or she can also be a Bachelor of Engineering.
    3. In order to apply for Indian Naval Academy, the candidate must possess an engineering degree from any recognized university or institute.

    NOTE Those who are in their final year of graduation can also apply for any of the three forces.
