After getting done with the graduation the very first question that strikes anyone’s mind is- what now? In fact, this question is not something that we ask over ourselves; the whole society has this question right on their very own faces the moment they discover you’re done with your graduation.

In order to satisfy this question, many of us think of going for a job for the sake of experience, money requirements etc. but simultaneously another option is also lingering around the head regarding post-graduation. Yes, many of us prefer getting done with our masers first and complete our education before jumping into the service sector. While listing the course for masters one of the widely opted courses is MBA (Masters in Business Administration). This is so because this is one of the most promising masters programmes that opens various job sectors for the student, especially when it is done with a recognised and reputed university or institute. It not only promises you the campus placement but also helps you make yourself dynamic enough to get fit into the fields of marketing, finance and human resource (majorly).

Not only the candidates who have done a B.Com. or B.B.A. but also the candidates who have done B.tech. apply for this course. So, if you are one of those students who are planning to pursue an M.B.A. as their masters and are reaching out for information regarding such examinations then, you’re at the very right place.

1. CAT:

CAT, also known as common admission test is one of the most competitive exams of India being conducted for inviting admissions in the top B-schools of India. It is quite tough as you go by the syllabus but obviously, students do get to clear this too. It is one of the most trusted exams which has its own reliability and credibility. After clearing all the rounds of CAT, the student can get admission in various IIMs of India (depending upon his performance in the exam). It is conducted by India Institute of Management. The application procedure usually begins by August and is done by September, the exam takes place near around November and the results are declared in January of the following year. Colleges that accept the admissions are- all the IIMs of India, TAPMI Manipal, IMI Delhi, MDI Gurgaon.

2. NMAT:

NMAT is conducted by the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC). They start inviting applications from September, application portal closes by November, exam is held in November (or January) and results are declared in around 3rd week of February. Colleges accepting admission through NMAT are- ICFAI Business Schools, NMIM, and Amity University.

3. XAT:

XAT stands for Xavier Aptitude Test. It is conducted by Xavier School of Management. The application process starts in the month of September, ends by November. The exams take place in early January of the following year and the results are declared by the end of January only. The colleges that accept admission through this exam are- BIMTECH, K J Somaiya, GIM Goa, XIMR, Loyola IBA, XBS, and XIME.

4. CMAT:

The conducting body of CMAT is All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). The applications are invited near around in the month of December and end in the same month only. The exam is held in January of the following year. The colleges where students clearing this exam can apply for are- JIM Noida, IISWBM, ISBS Pune, GLIM Chennai, DBS Dehradun.

5. GMAT:

GMAT stands for Graduate Management Aptitude Test. It is conducted by the Graduate Management Admission Council. The participating colleges for this exam are- FMS Delhi, GLIM Gurgaon, ISB, XLRI Jamshedpur, NISM Mumbai and a few more.

6. TANCET:

TANCET stands for Tamil Nadu Common Entrance Test, it is a state level MBA entrance exam. The conducting body of this exam is Anna University Tamil Nadu. The colleges that accept this test are from Tamil Nadu only, they are- SRM University, Chennai, Sona SOM Salem, Anna University, TSM Madurai, PSGIM and a few others. The applications are invited in the month of January (early) and end by the end of the same month. Exam is to be held in late February and results declared by the end of March.

7. SNAP:

Snap stands for Symbiosis National Aptitude Test. It is conducted by Symbiosis International University. The application portal opens up by the end of September and closes by November. The exam is held in December or January and the results are declared in January.

8. ATMA:

The conducting body for ATMA is Association of India Management Schools (AIMA). The application process starts in November and ends in February. The exam is conducted in January only and the results are declared in late February only.

9. IBSAT:

The conducting body for this exam is ICFAI Business Schools. The applications are invited in the month of June. The exam is conducted in the month of December and the results are declared in the month of January following that year.

10. MAH MBA CET:

It is conducted by the Directorate of Technical Education, Maharashtra. The application process starts by mid of January and ends by the mid of February. The exam is held in March and the results are declared by the end of March.

NOTE The given dates are tentative and are subjected to change on the discretion of the conducting bodies. Students are requested to keep a track of their official website in order to be in the mainstream of dates.

Apart from these there are also some MBA entrance exams that are state level and are mostly accepted by the colleges of that particular state only, they are- Karnataka PGCET, Maharashtra CET (MHT-CET), OJEE (Odisha Joint Entrance Exam), AP ICET (Andhra Pradesh ICET).

There are also some institute level exams that are conducted for MBA, they are-

  • IIFT MBA Entrance Exam which is conducted by India Institute of Foreign Trade.
  • TISSNET, conducted by Tata Institute of Social Sciences.
  • MICAT, conducted by MICA Ahmedabad.
  • SNAP, conducted by Symbiosis Institutes and also includes SCIT, SIBM, SIMS etc.
  • KIITEE, conducted by KIIT School of Management Bhubaneswar.
