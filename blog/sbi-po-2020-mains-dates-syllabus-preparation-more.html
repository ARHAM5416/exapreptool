SBI- PO is one of those exams which is written by a majority of Indian youth who wants to serve in the banking sector or simply wants to have a reputed job of Probationary Officer.

It is conducted every year depending upon the number of vacancies and is also one of the most promising exams IN terms of credibility and reliability. It also ensures a secured job to those who get to clear it and make their way all through the different stages of this exam.

Now, let’s talk about the important dates of SBI-PO:

Particulars Dates
Application form 14th November, 2020
Prelims exam 31stDecember, 2020 – 2nd, 4th ,5th
Prelims result January, 2021 3rd week of January,2021
Mains exam 29th January, 2021
Interview and Group Discussion February/ March, 2021
Final result declaration Last week of March,2021

The candidates who get to clear the prelims will be supposed to appear in mains which will be conducted in a short while in order to make their way to interview and group discussion.

Following are some of key points of this exam:

  • State Bank of India is the conducting body of this exam.
  • The official website is www.sbi.co.in
  • It is conducted once in a year.
  • It is conducted on a national level and invites applications from all over the country.
  • There are three stages in this exam, namely- prelims, mains, group discussion and interview.
  • Number of expected candidates who applied is approximately 10 lakh.
  • Seats available for this are 2,000.

    Syllabus for SBI-PO Mains:

  • Data Analysis and Interpretation:

    • Bar graphs, pie charts and line graphs (which could be double as well), mixed graphs-which are a combination of any of the mentioned graphs, radar chart, tabular graph, paragraph DI and missing graphs.

  • Computer Aptitude and Reasoning Ability:

    • Seating arrangements, puzzles, syllogism, inequality, coding-decoding, input-output, data sufficiency, reasoning, computer aptitude and miscellaneous topics.

  • General Awareness:

    • Static affairs, general banking and financial affairs and current affairs.

  • English Language:

    • Cloze test, reading comprehension, odd one out, para jumbles, and theme based questions, paragraph and statement completion, sentence connectors.

    • Preparation strategy for SBI-PO Mains:

  • Go through the syllabus–

    • The syllabus for this examination is relatively precise and limited but, the level of question being asked is not at all limited to any level. As an aspirant the first thing you must do is to note down the syllabus so that you can start the preparations in the right way. The syllabus will play the pivotal role around which your preparations are going to revolve.

  • Checkout the Exam Pattern–

    • The exam pattern plays a very important role in any of the examinations, be it of any sort. It’ll help you to discover various things about the exam like- mode of the exam, number of questions being asked, how many sections are there, how many questions will be asked from each section, what is the weightage given to each section, is there any negative marking etc.

  • Have a proper schedule:

    • This is an exam that you cannot pass without a proper strategy, routine and time-table. Yes, having a right and balanced time-table will not only allow you to keep everything within the desired time frame but will also keep a track of your preparations. By doing so you can easily divide how much time has to be devoted to which of the subjects. This will also complement your revision process.

  • Pick the right reference Books:

    • Books are the building blocks of your preparations. Without a book your preparations will be baseless. One thing that you should be very careful about is the quality of the book that you must buy. Yes, nowadays the books and study material is available in the market and on the internet in abundance, now it is your job to pick up refined and precise material only. Quality rated good books are your investments not just in the terms of money but also in the terms of time and efforts which will definitely be paid off at later stages of life.

  • Previous year Papers:

    • Previous year papers will work as the sculptor to your preparations and will prove to be complementary to your preparations. These question papers will carve out the extra knowledge which is not pretty much relevant to this examination and should be avoided for a while. Simultaneously, it’ll also unfold those nuances of the exam that you must be aware of beforehand. Previous year question papers are simply Bible to your preparations.

  • Mocks and Practice Sets:

    • Mocks and practice sets are something that will give you an edge over the rest of the competitors. It’ll allow you to know your strengths and weaknesses and will ensure that your preparations move ahead in a better shape. Things that you’ll get wrong in the mocks will be an opportunity to correct them before actually writing the exams and those that you got right will work as a morale booster.

    Hopefully, this was helpful for you.
