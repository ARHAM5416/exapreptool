GATE stands for Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering. It is conducted every year either by any of the IITs, namely- Bombay, Guwahati, Kanpur, Delhi, Kharagpur, Roorkee, Madras or by Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore (IISC Bangalore). It allows the students to get admission in postgraduate engineering and science courses. GATE is conducted on national level and is a gateway for Master of Engineering, Master in Technology, and also enables the student to apply for PhD in institutes like IITs, IIITs and NITs.

Professional institutes and societies:

There are 7 such institutes and societies that are also conducting examinations in associated fields of engineering. Those candidates who get to qualify any of such exams by any of the following given institutes or societies are also eligible to apply for MTech through Gate;

  • The Institute of Civil Engineers (ICE)
  • The Institution of Engineers, India (IE)
  • The Aeronautical Society of India (AeSI)
  • The Institute of Electronics and Telecommunication Engineers
  • The Indian Institute of Metals
  • The Indian Institute of Industrial Engineers (IIE)
  • The Indian Institute of Chemical Engineers, including Polymer and Environmental Group (IIChemE)

    • NOTE Engineering students who are not from India can also apply for GATE only after satisfying the concerned eligibility conditions which are mentioned on the official website.

    Exam Pattern for GATE 2021:

    The exam pattern mainly revolves around the points like- mode of examination, duration of examination, number of questions being asked, how many sections will be there, what type of questions will be there, what will be the distribution of marks and what will be the marking.

    Let us take up each one of these one by one:

    Mode of examination Computer based test
    Duration of examination 3 hours
    Number of subjects 27
    sections General aptitude and subject opted by the student
    Type of questions Multiples choice questions, Multiple select questions and Numerical answer type questions
    Number of questions 10 of General aptitude and 55 of concerned subject (total= 65)
    Marks distribution
  • General aptitude-15 marks
  • Engineering mathematics-13 marks
  • Subject questions-72 marks
  • Total- 100 marks

    Preparation Strategy for GATE 2021:

    By now we hope that you are well aware of the tentative dates of the examination and the one which is in the limelight is the date of examination, something that you should embrace yourself for and be prepared. We don’t want any of our students to be left behind. In fact this is the right time to kick-start your preparations with full persistence. GATE examination is not at all a piece of cake but, with correct strategy, guidance and knowledge you can definitely make your way through and can reach the other side of the story.

    Following are some of the empirically tested tips that you must follow in order to realise your dream:

    Know your Syllabus well–

    The syllabus for each and every subject is different but the part of the general aptitude is common. The first thing that you must do as an aspirant is to note down the syllabus and go through it thoroughly. Analysing the syllabus helps you design an outlay of your preparations and gives it a direction, so do it right now!

    Checkout the Exam Pattern–

    The exam pattern will allow you to know how the exam is set. It will help you in figuring out the type of questions and their pattern and will give you a good insight regarding attempting them the right way.

    Set a Timetable:

    Setting a time table will not only help you in keeping up with the track of time but will also prevent your preparation from falling off the brim of time limits. It’ll complement your strategy. Please remember that your timetable should allow you to have breaks as well, don’t be too harsh on yourself. So, go and make one and follow it religiously.

    Previous year Papers:

    Previous year question papers are complementary for your preparations. This is so because previous year question papers will not only give you an insight about the topics and questions which are being asked frequently but will also allow you to unfold the layers of topics which hold the utmost weight.

    Mocks and Practice Sets:

    Mocks and practice sets should be an integral part of your preparations as you’ll move towards the revision. Mocks and practice sets will enable you to discover your weak points on which you still need to build up and the strong points that are almost done and merely need a quick revision. Nowadays various online platforms are available which provide mocks, some are free and some are paid. Make sure you regularly take mocks. Another advantage of mocks and practice sets is that- you can have a taste of the real exam. Yes, it will prepare you to take the real test by making you go through the same sort of pressure under controlled situations. Take it as the trailer to the real picture!
