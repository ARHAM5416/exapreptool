What is CDS :

CDS stands for Combined Defence Services which is conducted twice in a year and is one of the best gateways to those who aspire to join Indian Defence. It is conducted by UPSC (Union Public Service Commission) and hence, the level of the exam is according to its level only. CDS opens the door to the prospective candidates to join any of the three armed forces of India, namely- Indian Army, Indian Airforce and Indian Navy, depending upon the choice s/he has filled and the marks the candidate has obtained.

Eligibility Criteria:

There are quite a few eligibilities that a candidate needs to satisfy before applying to the CDS, they are listed below

1. Nationality-

  • The candidate must be a citizen of India.
  • The candidate can be a subject of Nepal
  • The candidate can be a subject of Bhutan
  • The candidate can be a refugee residing in India before 1st of January, 1962 for permanent settlement.
  • If the candidate is a migrant from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka and East African countries then he compulsorily has to be a Person of Indian Origin.

    • If the candidate falls in other criteria apart from being a citizen of India then, he should possess a certificate in his favour of eligibility which must be issued by the Indian Government.

    2. Educational Qualification

    1. In order to apply for the India Military Academy and Officers’ Training Academy (OTA), the candidate must be a graduate from any recognised university or institute.
    2. In order to apply for Indian Airforce Academy, the candidate must be a graduate with Physics and Mathematics as her/his subjects in 12th or s/he must have acquired the Bachelor of Engineering.
    3. In order to apply for Indian Naval Academy, the candidate must possess an engineering degree from any recognised university or institute.

    Note: Those who are in their final year of graduation can also apply for any of the three forces.

    3. Age Limit and Marital Status

    Officers’ Training Academy (SSC Women Non-technical) 19-25 years Unmarried Women, Widows and Divorcees (must have divorce documents) who have not remarried and are issueless.
    Officers’ Training Academy (SSC Men) 19-25 years Unmarried
    Air Force Academy 19-25 years Candidates below the age of 25 must be unmarried and those who are above could be married but they will not be provided with any accommodation for their family during the training period nor will they be allowed to live with their family for the training period.
    Indian Naval Academy 19-25 years Unmarried
    Indian Military Academy 19-25 years Unmarried

    It is usually conducted twice a year during the months of February and September. There are approximately 400 vacancies released during an attempt. The exam is offline.

    Exam Pattern

    The exam has been divided into 3 shifts for IMA, AFA and INA but for OTA there are only two shifts. In both the exams the number of questions is 100 and yes, there is negative marking. Opting for a wrong question can make a candidate lose one-third of the marks per wrong question. The time allotted for each shift is 2 hours. All the questions will be MCQ type questions and an OMR sheet will be provided to the candidates wherein they will be required to fill the circle against the option they have chosen for the respective question. The medium of the paper is both Hindi and English hence, there is no issue of switching the language as the questions will be written in both the languages.

    Every section of the paper tests the candidate in different- different aspects like English majorly focuses on how well the candidate holds command over the language, the General Awareness is all about how well the candidate can remember the facts, the static G.K. and how well informed he is about his own surroundings and country. The mathematics portion moreover deals with the presence of mind and how well the candidate can apply his knowledge as and when required.

    After clearing the written exam the prospective candidates will be required to select an SSB centre for further proceedings of the recruitment process.
